Active Contained Landfills in the Commonwealth of Kentucky

Shelby Browning-University of Kentucky Geography Student

Kentucky has nearly 4.5 million residents within its borders, averaging about 110 people per square mile. Due to this, the Commonwealth must utilize many various waste disposal methods, including incineration, waste-to-energy, recycling, landfills, and many other options. In this map, I analyzed only currently active and operating contained landfills. This is a very important disctintion, as there are many different types of landfills, so this map only portrays a small fraction (and arguably the least potentially harmful fraction) of landfills in Kentucky. A majority of those landfills are deemed "Historic Landfills", which tend to fall outside the regulations of the EPA.

Contained landfills are the most familiar to the public and accept most of the communities' municipal solid waste. According to the Kentucky Energy and Environment Cabinet (KY EEC), "This category of solid waste site or facility is designed to accept all nonhazardous solid waste, including household, commercial and industrial waste. This waste may include shredded tires, household hazardous waste, limited quantity generator hazardous waste, and nonhazardous spill cleanup residue. These facilities are often able to accept special waste for disposal as well." Other types of landfill sites found in Kentucky include construction/demolition debris landfills, residual landfills, and special waste landfills.
